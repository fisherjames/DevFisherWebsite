Me

I’m consider myself a very curious person, and I have been interested in technology for as long as I remember. I’m currently studying Computer Science and I love it. CS is more than a degree for me, it’s my passion. The pace at which technology moves both excites and fascinates me. I enjoy trying to keep my finger on the pulse, despite how hard it can be.

Technology Challenge

I like to develop myself outside of my studies. For example, hackathons - like the University Technology Challenge that me and the rest of the Edge Hill team won, beating out 9 Universities to take first prize. We were tasked with designing a mobile application concept that met the requirements set out in the brief given to us from Barclays. We had to consider everything from budget constraints to risk assessments and demonstrate this in our proposal. We proposed an app that recognises products in an image and provides a link to purchase, as well as strategically offering discounts to promote local SMEs.

BBC Sport

I was lucky enough to undertake a 1 week placement with the Software Engineering team at BBC Sport in Salford. While I was already pretty certain I wanted to work as a Software Engineer this experience confirmed my expectations and convinced me to set my sights on this career with enthusiasm. I loved the work culture that I saw at BBC Sport and while I was clearly not experienced enough to offer my assistance at the time, I learned a lot from the engineers in terms of methodologies and best practices. They were also nice enough to give me tips on how best to develop my skillset, and break into the industry.

Student Mentor

Recently In between my classes I have been helping out the 1st year students in their Programming seminars. 1st years always prefer to talk to other students, and answering their questions about the basic concepts of OOP has really helped to solidify my own understanding. It’s easy to forget how difficult it is at the bottom of the learning curve, and I really enjoy helping people learn.

MTA Cert

I have recently passed the Microsoft 98-361 exam and been awarded the MTA (Software Development Fundamentals) Certification.

I took this exam because I would like to continue on the pathway and earn the Microsoft Certified Solutions Developer (App Builder) Certification, and also to see my progression.

TeaFresh

A big side project for me currently is building a business with a close friend of mine. We were lucky enough to be chosen to take part in a start-up business program at Edge Hill and we have been awarded £2000 (in tranches) to help us launch. From the glimmer of an idea, to researching the market, building a customer profile, and assessing all the potential risks. It has been tough at times but we are more excited than ever and can’t wait until we are ready to launch.
